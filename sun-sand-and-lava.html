The Journey

China's tropical south.

Hainan sits at the latitude of Hawaii, a green island in the South China Sea — and while the north of the country freezes, golf here is played in shirtsleeves through the depth of winter. It is China's most unlikely golf destination, and quietly its most rewarding: Blackstone at Mission Hills — World Top 100, host of The Match with Tiger Woods — Tom Weiskopf's seaside links on the dunes at Shenzhou Peninsula, and a headland round at Lu Hui Tou, the South China Sea on one side of the fairway and the mountain on the other.

And Hainan is more than its fairways. Down on the surf coast at Wanning, with our partner SurfChinaCo, complete beginners stand up on a real, rideable wave at a PerfectSwell pool — so the non-golfers come home with a story of their own.

The Golf

Three coasts, three kinds of golf.

Hainan holds more World Top 100 golf than anywhere else in China — volcanic-rock parkland, true seaside links and a headland course above Sanya Bay.

World Top 100
Haikou · Hainan

Mission Hills Haikou — Blackstone

The headline course at the world's largest golf resort, Blackstone is carved straight through a field of black volcanic rock — fairways framed by jagged lava walls, white sand and tropical palm. Both Tiger Woods and Rory McIlroy have played here, meeting in The Match at Mission Hills on the Blackstone course; it holds a place in the World Top 100 and remains the course every visiting golfer wants to tick off. Floodlit play and natural hot springs at the door make it the easy, spectacular start to a Hainan trip.

Shenzhou · Hainan

The Dunes at Shenzhou Peninsula

A natural links carved from real sand dunes along a wild, unspoilt stretch of tropical coast — closer in spirit to Ireland than the South China Sea. Tom Weiskopf routed the holes through tumbling duneland to the water's edge, leaving the wind and the land to do the defending. It is the most thrilling drive-and-pitch golf on the island, and the round most guests remember longest.

Sanya's headland
Lu Hui Tou Peninsula · Sanya

Lu Hui Tou Golf Club

Sanya's headland course — eighteen holes draped across the Lu Hui Tou peninsula between the South China Sea and the green mountain ridge. Nelson & Haworth's routing uses the elevation to give every approach a different angle and every tee a different view of Sanya Bay. Minutes from the Haitang Bay resorts, it makes the island's most polished golf day: the round in the morning, a yacht or the spa by mid-afternoon.

The classic shape is Blackstone to open, the Dunes for the round you'll talk about, and a headland day at Lu Hui Tou to finish in Sanya. Keen groups add a second loop at the Dunes; everyone else trades it for the surf coast and the sea.

After the Round

The day does not end at the 18th.

Hainan eats the way an island should: the morning's catch chosen from the tanks and grilled by the water, Wenchang chicken done as the island has cooked it for centuries, clay-pot rice in the port towns and cold local beer as the fishing boats come in. The best tables are set on the sand, a few steps from the tide.

For golfers, the evening becomes a seafood dinner at the water's edge — booked, briefed and waiting by the time you've putted out.

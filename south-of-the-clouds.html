The Journey

China, south of the clouds.

Yúnnán means "south of the clouds," and the province keeps the promise of the name. The route runs from Kunming, the City of Eternal Spring, north along the old Tea Horse Road to Lijiang, where Naxi rooftops crowd beneath Jade Dragon Snow Mountain — and beyond, to the highland plain they renamed Shangri-La. No region of China feels less like the China you imagined.

And the golf is unlike anywhere else in the game. Spring City's two courses rank among Asia's very best, laid over a highland lake at 2,000 metres. At Jade Dragon, you play the world's longest championship course beneath a 5,500-metre glacier — where the air is thin enough that the ball flies ten percent farther, and you'll need it.

At a Glance

  • Two rounds at Spring City — Nicklaus' Mountain and Trent Jones Jr's Lake
  • Golf beneath a 5,500 m glacier at Jade Dragon Snow Mountain
  • Lijiang's UNESCO old town in the quiet hours, with a Naxi guide
  • The Tea Horse Road: aged pu'er, courtyard tea houses, mountain villages
  • Dawn prayers with the monks at Songzanlin, Shangri-La
  • Hotels the likes of Banyan Tree Lijiang, the snow mountain at the window
  • Private driver, guide and every tee time handled

The Golf

Golf at altitude.

Yunnan's two great venues hold World Top 100 and Asia Top 50 rankings between them — highland golf in thin, clear air, with scenery no other golf destination can answer.

World Top 100
Yangzong Lake · Kunming

Spring City Golf & Lake Resort

The crown jewel of Chinese golf: two courses ranked among the continent's finest, draped over hills above a highland lake at 2,000 metres. Robert Trent Jones Jr's Lake Course tumbles toward the water through forests of eucalyptus and pine — a World Top 100 fixture — while Jack Nicklaus' Mountain Course climbs the ridgeline above it, all elevation change and long views. The eternal-spring climate means both play beautifully in any month of the year.

Golf at 3,100 m
Lijiang · Yulong

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

The most dramatic setting in golf. At 3,100 metres in the Himalayan foothills, Jade Dragon stretches to 8,548 yards — the longest championship course in the world — yet the thin air carries your ball ten percent farther, and you will want every metre of it. Above the fairways the 5,500-metre glacier of the snow mountain fills the sky; around them, pine forest and Naxi villages. A round here is simply unrepeatable anywhere else.

The classic shape is two rounds at Spring City and one beneath the snow mountain — Lijiang is 45 minutes from Kunming by air. Keen golfers add a second round at Jade Dragon; the morning light on the glacier argues for it.

After the Round

The day does not end at the 18th.

Evenings in Yunnan belong to the courtyard: wild-mushroom feasts in season, Naxi family recipes cooked over fire, aged pu'er poured by a charcoal hearth as the old town's lanterns come on.

For golfers, the round gives way to the tables worth crossing the old town for — private courtyards, a chef who knows the morning's market — booked, briefed and waiting by the time you've putted out.

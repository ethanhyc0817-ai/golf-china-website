The Journey

China's tropical south.

Hainan sits at the latitude of Hawaii, a green island floating in the South China Sea — and while the north of the country freezes, here the palms barely stir, the water stays warm, and golf is played in shirtsleeves through the depth of winter. It is China's most unlikely golf destination, and quietly its most rewarding: a coast of sand dunes, lava rock and turquoise bays that has drawn the game's great architects to build some of the finest courses in Asia.

The golf alone would justify the journey. Mission Hills Haikou's Blackstone course — laid through black volcanic rock and host to The Match with Tiger Woods — sits in the World Top 100. Tom Weiskopf's Dunes at Shenzhou Peninsula is true seaside links, raw and wind-blown along a wild stretch of coast. And on a remote headland to the southeast, Coore & Crenshaw's Shanqin Bay keeps no public tee sheet at all — the most exclusive and private club in Asia, played by invitation only.

But Hainan is more than its fairways. Down on the island's surf coast at Wanning, in partnership with SurfChinaCo, we put complete beginners onto a real, rideable wave — at a PerfectSwell wave pool where the swell is built to order, the same gentle peel every time. It is the rare golf trip where the non-golfers, the children and the first-timers come home with a story of their own.

The Golf

Three coasts, three kinds of golf.

Hainan holds more World Top 100 golf than anywhere else in China — volcanic-rock parkland, true seaside links and a clifftop course that almost no one is allowed to play.

World Top 100
Haikou · Hainan

Mission Hills Haikou — Blackstone

The headline course at the world's largest golf resort, Blackstone is carved straight through a field of black volcanic rock — fairways framed by jagged lava walls, white sand and tropical palm. Both Tiger Woods and Rory McIlroy have played here, meeting in The Match at Mission Hills on the Blackstone course; it holds a place in the World Top 100 and remains the course every visiting golfer wants to tick off. Floodlit play and natural hot springs at the door make it the easy, spectacular start to a Hainan trip.

Shenzhou · Hainan

The Dunes at Shenzhou Peninsula

A natural links carved from real sand dunes along a wild, unspoilt stretch of tropical coast — closer in spirit to Ireland than the South China Sea. Tom Weiskopf routed the holes through tumbling duneland to the water's edge, leaving the wind and the land to do the defending. It is the most thrilling drive-and-pitch golf on the island, and the round most guests remember longest.

Wanning · Hainan

Shanqin Bay

Coore & Crenshaw on a wild, remote Hainan headland — and the most exclusive golf club in Asia. Clifftop holes, native sand and constant sea wind have earned it a standing place in the World Top 100, yet almost no one has played it: the club keeps no public tee sheet, and its privacy is the point. We arrange guest play here selectively, by invitation, and only where it can genuinely be confirmed — never sold as a certainty.

At Mission Hills · Haikou

Stay where the volcanoes meet the springs

For the Blackstone rounds, the move is to stay inside Mission Hills itself — where the first tee is minutes from your door and the day ends in the resort's natural volcanic hot springs, the rock still warm beneath the water.

It is the easiest, most self-contained golf stay on the island: thirty-six holes, mineral springs and dinner, with nothing more to arrange than when you'd like to play.

The Journey

Three cities. Four rounds. One unforgettable introduction.

There is a right way to see China for the first time, and it runs from north to south — from the capital of emperors, through the city of the future, to the lake the poets never stopped painting. This journey holds all three, stitched together by high-speed rail, and threads four of the country's finest golf courses through the icons in between.

Beijing opens the trip the way it has opened China for six centuries: the Forbidden City before the crowds, the hutong lanes where the city still lives, and two rounds in the mountains north of the capital — Reignwood Pine Valley, Jack Nicklaus fairways laid beneath the Great Wall itself at a club whose membership is otherwise closed to the world, and Topwin on Yanqi Lake, where President Obama teed it up beneath the Yanshan range.

Then the country changes around you. Four hours south by rail, Shanghai delivers the counterpoint: the Bund, the old concessions, some of the best tables in the world — and a wild links among the dunes of the Yangtze estuary that feels closer to Ireland than to anything else in Asia. The finale is gentler still: an hour further south, Hangzhou's West Lake at dawn, Longjing tea on the hillside, and a closing round at Fuchun Golf Club, the only course in Asia routed through a tea plantation.

Day by Day

The shape of the journey.

Nine nights, three cities, four rounds — a rhythm of golf, icons and unhurried time that has been refined across many first visits. Yours will be redrawn around your dates and your party.

BeijingNights 1–4
Day 1 Arrive Beijing

Landing in the capital of emperors.

Met on arrival with fast-track clearance and a private transfer into the old city, where your hotel waits among the hutong lanes a few streets from the Forbidden City. The evening is deliberately gentle: a first walk through the grey-brick alleys as the lanterns come on, and a welcome dinner of crisp-skinned Peking duck at a courtyard table.

Private arrival & fast-trackWelcome dinner
Day 2 Imperial Beijing

Through the Meridian Gate.

Into the Forbidden City with an expert English-speaking historian who reads the architecture like a text — six centuries of dynastic history brought to life hall by hall. The afternoon stays imperial: the overlook from Jingshan's peak, then the Summer Palace by boat as the light softens over Kunming Lake.

Forbidden City, privately guidedSummer Palace by boat
Day 3 Round One

Golf beneath the Great Wall.

The round the journey is built around. At Reignwood Pine Valley, Nicklaus fairways climb through the Jundu Mountains with the Wall tracing the ridgeline above the back nine — a closely-held private club where we arrange guest play on your behalf, with caddies, forecaddies and a quiet clubhouse lunch beneath the château's chandeliers.

Golf · Reignwood Pine ValleyClubhouse lunch
Day 3 The Great Wall

The Wall at Juyongguan, without the crowds.

Twenty-five minutes from your final putt, the Wall rides the mountain pass at Juyongguan. While the famous tourist sections fill with tour buses, years on the ground have taught us exactly which stretch to climb, and at which hour — ramparts all but to yourselves, the light going gold over the Jundu Mountains.

Great Wall · Juyongguan25 min from Reignwood
Day 4 Round Two

Lakeside golf beneath the Yanshan range.

A second morning of mountain golf, this time at Topwin Golf & Country Club on Yanqi Lake — the Woosnam course played by President Obama, its fairways running along the waters where the world's leaders gathered for the 2014 APEC summit. Back in the city by mid-afternoon with the evening free.

Golf · TopwinYanqi Lake
Day 4 Hutong Beijing

A hutong culture and food evening.

Back from the mountains, the rest of the day belongs to the grey-brick lanes where the capital actually lives: the alleys around the Drum and Bell Towers with the people who grew up in them, ending at one of the courtyard chef's tables Beijing's food-lovers keep to themselves.

Drum & Bell TowersCourtyard chef's table
Day 5 Beijing → Shanghai

South, at 350 kilometres an hour.

A last unhurried morning — the Temple of Heaven at opening time, or nothing at all — then the business-class cabin of a high-speed train south. Four and a half hours later the skyline of Pudong rises over the Huangpu, and your hotel on the Bund is pouring the first drink of the second act. Dinner above the lights of two skylines.

Day 6 Round Three

A wild links at the mouth of the Yangtze.

An hour from the Bund, on Chongming Island, the trip's biggest surprise: Yangtze Dunes, a true links through native dunes and wetland, firm turf and river wind, ranked among China's top three modern courses. We shape it in the weeks before you fly — or leave it gloriously empty.

Day 8 Shanghai → Hangzhou

An hour south, a thousand years back.

A last Shanghai morning, then a short high-speed hop south into Jiangnan — the land "south of the river". By late afternoon you are on West Lake by private boat, mist on the water and the Leifeng Pagoda catching the last light, with dinner at a lakeside table the locals guard jealously.

High-speed rail, 1 hWest Lake by private boat
Day 9 Round Four

Golf through a tea plantation.

The closing round is the gentlest and the most unusual: Fuchun Golf Club, the only course in Asia routed through the rolling hills of a working Longjing tea plantation, its greens mirrored in still water below grey-tiled pavilions. Tea picked a few metres from the fairway follows the round; the evening is West Lake's.

The Golf

Four rounds, four different Chinas.

Mountain golf beneath the Wall, lakeside fairways at Yanqi Lake, a wild river links and a course threaded through tea terraces — the strongest possible first hand of golf in China.

Round 1 · Signature
Jundu Mountains · Beijing

Reignwood Pine Valley

Set beneath the Great Wall near Badaling, Reignwood Pine Valley is one of China's most private and prestigious golf clubs — a Nicklaus mountain-parkland layout where elevated tees, sculpted bunkering and fast, guarded greens unfold below the Jundu Mountains, the Wall tracing the ridgeline above. Membership is closely held; we arrange guest play on your behalf.

Round 3 · Wild links
Chongming Island · Shanghai

Yangtze Dunes

An hour from the Bund, on an island at the mouth of the Yangtze, lies the most surprising round in China — a true links running through native dunes and wetlands, shaped by the celebrated Australian firm OCM. Firm turf, river wind, blind shoulders and greens tucked into the sand: golf that feels closer to the Irish coast than to anything else in Asia.

Round 4 · Tea plantation
Fuchun River Valley · Hangzhou

Fuchun Golf Club

Six hundred years ago the Fuchun valley inspired one of China's greatest paintings; today it holds the only course in Asia routed through the rolling hills of a tea plantation. Fairways sweep beneath terraced walls of Longjing tea, greens sit mirrored in still water below the resort's grey-tiled pavilions, and the mist comes and goes all morning. Golf found nowhere else in the game — the perfect closing note.

Day by Day

The shape of the journey.

Sixteen nights, seven rounds, four regions — golf and icons in alternation, with air kept in the schedule so neither ever feels rushed.

BeijingNights 1–3
Day 1 Arrive Beijing

Landing in the capital of emperors.

Arrive in Beijing, where you are met with fast-track clearance and privately transferred to your hotel a few streets from the Forbidden City. Gather for a welcome dinner of crisp-skinned Peking duck at a courtyard table in the hutong lanes.

Private arrival & fast-trackWelcome dinner
Day 2 Imperial Beijing

Through the Meridian Gate.

Explore the Forbidden City with an expert English-speaking historian who brings six centuries of dynastic history to life hall by hall. Take in the whole palace from Jingshan's peak, then cross the Summer Palace's Kunming Lake by boat.

Forbidden City, privately guidedSummer Palace by boat
Day 3 Round One

Golf beneath the Great Wall — then the Wall itself.

Play Reignwood Pine Valley, a closely-held private club where Nicklaus fairways climb through the Jundu Mountains with the Wall tracing the ridgeline above — we arrange guest play on your behalf. Then climb the Wall itself at Juyongguan, twenty-five minutes away, on the quietest stretch of ramparts as the light goes gold.

Golf · Reignwood Pine ValleyGreat Wall · Juyongguan

Topwin on Yanqi Lake, where Obama played.

Day 4 Beijing → Shanghai

South, at 350 kilometres an hour.

Visit the Temple of Heaven, then board the business-class cabin of a high-speed train south to Shanghai. Settle into your hotel on the historic Bund and dine above the lights of two skylines.

High-speed rail, business classThe Bund after dark
Day 5 Round Two

A wild links at the mouth of the Yangtze.

Play Yangtze Dunes on Chongming Island — a true links through native dunes and wetland, ranked among China's top three modern courses, the world's busiest shipping lane gliding past beyond the dunes. Back in the city, walk beneath the plane trees of the old French Concession.

Golf · Yangtze DunesFrench Concession evening
Day 6 Shanghai, Your Way

The city, arranged around you.

A day built to order: contemporary art in the West Bund museums, a tailor's fitting on the Bund, architecture from art-deco to the 632-metre Shanghai Tower, or the city's best kitchens back to back. Keen golfers spend it at Sheshan International instead.

Fully tailored dayOr add tournament golf

Play China's most famous course, host of its flagship world-tour event for two decades — the closing stretch around the old quarry has decided more champions than any other holes in Asia. A strictly private members' club; we arrange your game, your caddies and lunch on the trophy terrace.

Day 7 Shanghai → Hangzhou

An hour south, a thousand years back.

Travel an hour south by high-speed rail into Jiangnan — the land "south of the river". Cross West Lake by private boat, the Leifeng Pagoda catching the last light, and dine at a lakeside table the locals guard jealously.

High-speed rail, 1 hWest Lake by private boat

The Golf

Seven rounds. The whole of golf in China.

Nicklaus fairways beneath the Great Wall, a wild river links, tea-terrace golf, the world's longest course beneath a glacier, a World Top 100 lakeside, a lava field and a seaside links to finish — no single journey holds a stronger hand.

Round 1 · Signature
Jundu Mountains · Beijing

Reignwood Pine Valley

Set beneath the Great Wall near Badaling, Reignwood Pine Valley is one of China's most private and prestigious golf clubs — a Nicklaus mountain-parkland layout where elevated tees, sculpted bunkering and fast, guarded greens unfold below the Jundu Mountains, the Wall tracing the ridgeline above. Membership is closely held; we arrange guest play on your behalf.

Round 2 · Wild links
Chongming Island · Shanghai

Yangtze Dunes

An hour from the Bund, on an island at the mouth of the Yangtze, lies the most surprising round in China — a true links running through native dunes and wetlands, shaped by the celebrated Australian firm OCM. Firm turf, river wind, blind shoulders and greens tucked into the sand: golf that feels closer to the Irish coast than to anything else in Asia.

Fuchun River Valley · Hangzhou

Fuchun Golf Club

Six hundred years ago the Fuchun valley inspired one of China's greatest paintings; today it holds the only course in Asia routed through the rolling hills of a tea plantation. Fairways sweep beneath terraced walls of Longjing tea, greens sit mirrored in still water below the resort's grey-tiled pavilions, and the mist comes and goes all morning. Golf found nowhere else in the game.

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain

The most dramatic setting in golf. At 3,100 metres, Jade Dragon stretches to 8,548 yards — the world's longest championship course — yet the thin air carries your ball ten percent farther. Above the fairways, the 5,500-metre glacier fills the sky; we build in two nights of acclimatisation before you play.
